2001 was the first year we put up an animated (computer controlled) display. All animated lights were controlled by 16 circuits via my [original parallel port control box](/technology/parallel-8/). This was also the first year for the new house!

## The Layout

- The **Star** took its position in the window above the door.
- Red rope lights ran down the edge of the drive way. (Always on)
- In the edge of the yard (left when facing the road) a "Merry Christmas" sign sitting just off the ground at a 90 degree angle to the road. The sign was about 1 foot tall and made of lights.
- The bushes were divided into three groups with each group being wired across the main door (i.e., the two spiral bushes on each side of the door were a group, and then the next couple of outer bushes to each side of the door were another group, etc.) Each group could be either red, blue, or green (or a mixture of the three). 3×3 = 9 circuits.
- One circuit was configured to light up all white lights in the bushes.
- White icicle lights were mounted in the gutters on a switchable circuit.
- Each of the 5 windows on the top floor were outlined in rope light, each on its own individual circuit.

## The Patterns

- **Scrolling Bushes**: Bushes scroll inwards using different colors
- **Dancing Bushes**: Bushes change colors outwards using Green, Red, Blue, Green-Blue, Green-Red, Red-Blue, and Red-Green-Blue.
- **Window Dance**: The 5 windows do a back and forth pattern as the pattern gets faster and faster.
- **White Flash**: The bushes and windows/icicle lights flash back and forth faster and faster.
